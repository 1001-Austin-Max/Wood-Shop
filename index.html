Welcome To The Wood Shop

The Art of Wood Working

Wood working is the craft of using wood to create furniture, bowls, chairs, and much more. Being proficient at using wood in a design sense requires: patience, attention to detail, and a willingness to learn. Patience is very important in the wood shop because it allows the craftsman to not rush to a finished project, but to use all the resources and skills available to them to create the best product possible. This brings us to the next skill: attention to detail. Wood can be very tricky to manipulate and without a keen eye, a finished project's imperfections can pile up. Finally, a key aspect in mastery of the wood-shop is an ability to learn. No one is immune from mistakes and it's important to recognize mistakes in wood-working to learn and adapt in the next project

Starting a Wood Project

When you have an idea for a new project that gets you excited the first thing you want to do is start using the tools. However, it's important to first go through the design process. During this process, you should:

  1. Create an initial drawing or sketch
  2. Plan out the dimensions of your project and what wood you'll use
  3. Make a final design
  4. Make a cut list
  5. Make a step by step plan of what you'll do until the end
